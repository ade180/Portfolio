natural and otherwise

Dagenham, London · Summer 2026

Subject: The unexpected intersection of ecological and computational systems.

I'm a Business Computing student at Birkbeck, London, with early experience at KPMG, PwC and Lloyds Bank and a founder's perspective from running Axxdea. — a small digital products business I built alongside my studies.

I came to environmental technology the way most genuine interests arrive: gradually, then all at once. A semester studying systems analysis. A summer actually growing tomatoes. The dawning realisation that an ecosystem and a well-designed software system have more in common than most people notice — feedback loops, emergent behaviour, the fragility that comes from over-optimising one variable at the expense of everything else.

"What would software look like if it was built for ecosystems rather than just about them?"

That question drives the work I'm building now. Garden management systems. Ecological monitoring dashboards. Databases of wild plants. Tools for people who want to understand the natural world with the same rigour they'd apply to a business problem.

I'm also pragmatic: I have strong frontend skills, genuine Big Four experience, and a systems analysis grade that I'm proud of. I'm looking for roles where all of that is useful — AgriTech, environmental consulting, sustainability technology, or anything at that intersection.
